Conclusion

It seems that GDP has the strongest correlation with life expectancy compared to the other variables measured. While GDP, schooling, and alcohol consumption all correlated with increase life expectancy, this result can change depending on if you are looking only and developing countries or not in the case of alcohol. Within the two catagories of developing and developed countries, there is a wide range in life expectancies. Developing countries had a very wide range although that was due in part to a miss-classification of France and Finland. Still, even when looking at countries individually there are wide ranges, with some countries experiencing dips and spikes in life expectancy due to events that occurred that year.
